## 1. What this document does well enough to become the root

Its composition discipline is exactly right: it never overrides the three specs beneath it, states their authority explicitly, and adds only what they do not cover. Section 0.1 deserves particular respect: rather than faking completeness across a hundred thousand words, it states its coverage allocation openly, gives full developer output to the flagship and one representative feature per module, and makes the fifteen-part template of 15.10 the honest contract for the long tail, with the definition of done in 15.9 (walls checked not assumed, estimates labeled with grades, no endpoint without a named caller, generative outputs grounded and reproducible) as the enforcement. The eight platform walls, W1 through W8, give the doctrine its best form yet: numbered, referenced throughout, and inherited by every feature, and this review recommends promoting the W-numbering into the canon ledger and the working vocabulary, so a finding can cite W2 the way a suite cites an assertion. Three design decisions merit singling out. The grounding validator on the Executive Briefing is the strongest generative-AI discipline in the stream: a sentence that cannot resolve to a fact row or an insight does not ship, the brief is stored with source references and auditable line by line, and the brief is required to include where the organization is improving, not only alarms. The Digital Twin ships as a deterministic Tier 0 engine whose elasticities are transparent, sampled, graded, and challengeable, with the learned twin dark, which is the only honest way to sell simulation. And the Graph distinguishes observed-correlation edges from validated-relationship edges, grades discoveries no higher than Inferred until the closed loop confirms them, and logs the wall filter's cuts so it is provable that no wall-crossing path was served. The shadow-column rule (every predictive column exists now, computed dark, so a flag flip is configuration rather than release) and the tier sentence (tiers gate capability, never safety) both enter the canon as written.

## 2. The sharp collision: the AI avatar speaks to the worker

Section 6.1 makes the daily check-in an AI avatar interaction: the avatar conducts the check-in inside the app. The locked law, adopted in the blueprint from the v2 rule and reaffirmed in the SIGMA review, is that nothing generative speaks to a worker: AI drafting is coordinator-facing and human-approved before any worker sees derived content, precisely because a generative system responding to a hurting worker's symptom description in its own words is one unlucky sentence away from clinical advice, device territory, and a trust failure. The repair preserves Craig's product intent completely and costs nothing he actually needs: the shipping avatar is a scripted, deterministic conversational rendering of the check-in schema, a guided flow whose every prompt and every branch is authored, reviewed copy (Calliope's lane, at grade 7, under the vocabulary laws), which delivers the conversational warmth and the thirty-second capture without a generative model in the loop. It collects and routes per program rules; it never advises, reassures diagnostically, or improvises. A genuinely generative conversational layer, if ever wanted, stages exactly like every other regulated capability: designed, built dark behind its own flag, and enabled only through governance. One paragraph in 6.1.1 makes this explicit, and the worker check-in v2 build already queued absorbs the scripted-avatar framing without rework.

## 3. The specification gap, formally assigned

The master names seventeen intelligence surfaces; the CRTW PRD specifies thirteen at widget depth. The four beyond it (Supervisor Intelligence, Job Task Intelligence expanded, Organizational Health, and the Benchmarking, Heat Map and Predictive layers as first-class surfaces) exist only at catalogue depth, which is exactly what Section 0.1 promises, but a catalogue entry is not buildable, and the gap should be an assignment, not an ambiency: each of the four is written to the 15.10 template, at CRTW-PRD depth, before its phase begins. One element inside that assignment needs a rule now: the Organizational Health Index that headlines the executive surface is a composite score sitting where decisions follow, so the explainability law applies to it in full: its formula, components, and weights are displayed wherever the index is, or it does not ship, because an unexplained organizational score is the same sin as an unexplained worker score, one level up.
