**Source:** Craig's Enterprise Integration Blueprint, Continuum and SIGMA-RH, prepared July 20, 2026: the layered architecture, the asymmetric data contract, the tiered intelligence engine, the eleven-view experience map, the compliance mapping, the competitive read, and the ten-year vision.
**Verdict in one line:** this is the strongest strategy document the venture holds, adopt it as the integration bible with one sharp repair (the write-back vocabulary must pass the same law as the employer serializer), a handful of blueprint deltas, and its open dependencies folded into the decision register.
**Date:** July 20, 2026

## 1. What this document gets right, and why the review is short on objections

It was written against the locked doctrine and it shows: inform-never-decide is stated as the governing constraint that overrides the document itself; the physician-guarded state machine, the employer serializer gate, the board submit-and-track posture, the proxy path, the no-video position, the dark tiers behind flags, and Canadian residency all appear exactly as the canon holds them. The asymmetry thesis is the document's best strategic sentence: consume a shallow reference set, return intelligence the record cannot compute, and the system of record wants the connection because it makes their data more valuable. The honest-API-reality section deserves specific praise: designing to the ideal, degrading to Power Automate and file exchange, and keeping the pilot's manual export in force means no promise in this document depends on an unconfirmed vendor capability, and both unknowns (REST API, SSO federation) are carried as named dependencies rather than assumptions. The threat picture is honest where sales documents usually are not: the white space is bracketed by Sword-Kaia on one side and Cority and Intelex on the other, with a quarterly reassessment instruction. Adopt all of it.

## 2. The one sharp repair: write-back vocabulary is employer-domain vocabulary

Section 2.2 sends Continuum outcomes into SIGMA-RH: functional recovery milestones, modified-duty participation, projected return-to-work windows, and escalation alerts. SIGMA-RH is the employer's system, read by employer eyes, so every field that crosses that boundary lives in the employer domain, and the law that governs the employer dashboard governs the write-back identically. Two fields need their wording repaired before they become contract language. Escalation alerts must arrive in SIGMA-RH as functional workflow states (case under clinical review per program rules, duty plan under review), never as escalation, regression, plateau, or any phrasing that lets an employer infer clinical trajectory from the claim record; an intelligence label inside SIGMA is the same leak as an intelligence label on the dashboard, wearing an integration costume. And projected return-to-work windows must carry their provenance in the field itself (clinician-reviewed estimate), because a bare date in a claim system reads as fact, gets planned against, and becomes an implied promise the product law forbids. The concrete obligation: the employer serialization CI test from the blueprint's own Section 7.1 extends to the SIGMA write-back adapter, asserting zero clinical fields, zero intelligence vocabulary, and provenance labels on every estimate, on that channel exactly as on the dashboard payloads. One sentence added to Section 2.2 fixes it, and the test makes it permanent.

## 3. What is genuinely new, and what it obligates

Four things enter the canon through this document. First, the anchor facts: SIGMA-RH as GardaWorld's confirmed system of record across 35,000 plus Canadian employees, the five-module OHS suite, Azure with per-customer isolation and Canadian residency, and the connector-not-API reality. Second, the occupational intelligence database stated with numbers for the first time in this stream: 45 positions, 50 tasks across 27 demand factors, 14 injuries with recovery-window configuration, 209 pre-matched duties across 26 categories, and the 22-code restriction taxonomy: this is the moat argument made concrete, and those numbers should join the canon ledger so every surface and suite that ever cites them agrees. Third, the deterministic match engine specified as an algorithm (incompatible codes block outright, occasional-demand conflicts mark conditional with the modification surfaced, ranking by essential-demand coverage weighted by duty class): this is buildable as written, and it upgrades the demo's Scene 8 vignette from illustration to working engine. Fourth, two new views: the SIGMA administrator (the customer-side integration contract as configuration: which fields flow, which write back, identity mapping), which is central to enterprise trust and belongs in the blueprint's role map, and the occupational health view aligned to SIGMA-RH Medical Clinics without duplicating the medical record. The role map grows from nine views to eleven, and because roles are data, both are inserts, not refactors.

## 4. Blueprint deltas and the decision register

Prompt 21 absorbs this document with four amendments. The integration architecture section gains the asymmetric contract and the three-state degradation (ideal, pragmatic, pilot) as its SIGMA chapter. The role map adds the two views above. The entity model gains the occupational database entities (position, task, demand factor, restriction code, duty compatibility, recovery-window configuration) as first-class tables, since the match engine queries them. And F4, the projection law, gains the write-back clause from Section 2 of this review. The decision register grows by three: D9, confirm SIGMA-RH REST or SOAP API beyond the Power Automate connector; D10, confirm SAML or Entra federation; D11, obtain the SOC 2 report and ISO 27001 certificate in diligence rather than from vendor pages, all three lifted directly from the document's own appendix, which did the register's work for it. None of the new decisions blocks the pilot, exactly as the document designed.

## 5. Tier language, held to its own discipline

The tier structure is right: Tier 0 as clinician-reviewed estimates and a deterministic query, Tier 1 as the machine engine shipped dark pending the Health Canada pathway, with the April 2026 machine-learning device guidance correctly in view. Two register notes keep it airtight. The worker check-in's optional guided-movement input is described with its consent and review guards, and it should also carry its flag status explicitly wherever it appears (the pilot ships with the flag off), so no reader mistakes a designed capability for a shipped one. And the ten-year financial layer, premium impact, rebate optimization, duration forecasting, is the one section whose vocabulary will drift into promised outcomes if marketing touches it unsupervised; the informed-confidence law and Calliope's no-promised-outcomes rule apply to every derivative of Section 9.5, and the ROI language should always be estimates with visible inputs, which the document's own Leadership view already words correctly.
