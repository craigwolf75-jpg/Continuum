# Continuum Plain Language Standard

The house content law for every user-facing word on the platform. Established by Prompt 33. Calliope authors to it; Argus patrols against it; the dash audit and the six-portal canon suite gate every change. See specs/CONTINUUM_PROMPT_33.md.

## 1. Reading level

All user-facing copy reads at Grade 6 to 8. The worker app is the strictest surface and holds the line first, but employer, HSE, board, and the user-facing parts of the admin surface all meet it too. Short sentences, common words, one idea per sentence.

## 2. Abbreviations expand at first use

Every abbreviation is written out the first time it appears on a surface, with the short form in parentheses, and the short form may be used after that on the same surface. Examples: Return to Work (RTW), then RTW; business to business (B2B); text message (SMS); Corporate Recovery, Training and Workforce Intelligence (CRTW). A bare abbreviation with no expansion anywhere on the surface is a violation.

## 3. No software terminology on user surfaces

User surfaces never show code tokens or engineering idiom. Banned on user copy: field or column names as written in code (for example entered_by_role), and words like API, endpoint, payload, retrieval, serializer, null, OTP, and nudge. Use the plain equivalent that names the real thing: "saved as a coordinator entry" not entered_by_role; "sign-in code" not OTP; "reminder" not nudge; "no way for software to pull the data back out" not retrieval API.

## 4. Meaning is never lost

Simplify the words, never the substance. Every functional, medical, legal, and privacy meaning stays exact after a rewrite. A plain sentence that changes what a field measures, weakens a privacy wall, or softens a legal obligation is a defect, not a clarity win. When in doubt, keep the meaning and find plainer words around it.

## 5. Canon phrases are exempt and stay verbatim

Certain phrases are asserted verbatim by the suites and carry legal or safety weight; they are never reworded: the compliance guard modal wording (Health Canada, four independent gates), and the escalation routing phrase (escalated to the clinician per program rules). These already read plainly and answer who and why; leave them exactly as written.

## 6. Clinician and counsel gated (do not reword without sign-off)

Four kinds of wording are named, not touched, because rewording them is a clinical or legal decision, not a copy decision: restriction display strings anywhere (clinician sign-off required); Form 7 field language (with counsel under Addendum A section A.3, where the wall wins); the pilot consent text (counsel, the July 21 to 22 critical-path item); and clinical-dashboard wording beyond an incidental tag, because a provider surface may keep clinical precision by design.

## 7. Enforcement

The dash audit and the six-portal canon suite run on every change. Calliope owns authoring to this standard; Argus patrols for drift. Any new user-facing string is written to this standard before it ships.
