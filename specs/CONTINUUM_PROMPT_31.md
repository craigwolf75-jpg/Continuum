## 1. The operating model: coordinator as hub

The demo family artifacts are single-device working tools, not a networked system: each portal persists in its own browser, and the worker bridge synchronizes tabs on one machine, not phones to offices. The pilot's network is therefore the coordinator: workers report by phone, text-prompted call, or in person; the coordinator enters what they report into the HSE portal on the coordinator machine, flagged as proxy entry, which is not a workaround but the architecture the doctrine already blessed: the proxy path exists precisely so the program runs fully for workers without the app. The clinician is reached by phone and fax as today, restrictions are keyed in as received (the clinician decides, the coordinator records), and the employer's questions are answered from the coordinator's screen in functional vocabulary. ## 3. The manual processes, defined

Enrollment and consent: paper consent using counsel-reviewed text, then a New Case entry; the consent text is the critical-path item for July 21 to 22 and is named in the pre-flight list below. Daily rhythm: a neutral reminder inside quiet hours (8am to 9pm, no case content in any message), the worker reports, the coordinator logs the proxy check-in; a pain of 8 or a red-flag report escalates per program rules and the coordinator phones the clinic. Clinical loop: assessments arrive by fax or phone, the coordinator keys restrictions as received with the review date, and only a communicated clinician decision moves a status. Duties: matched from the employer's approved list, hazard checked by a person on site, assigned in the portal, acknowledged verbally each morning with the coordinator noting it until check-in v2 ships the tap. Ontario filing: the employer files the Form 7 within three business days of learning of the injury through WSIB eServices on the human path, the coordinator tracks the deadline and the acknowledgement in the board portal artifact, and the B2B adapter is the Phase 2 upgrade the matrix confirmed. Weekly: the coordinator reads the pilot instrumentation from HSE analytics (time to first suitable duty, step-ups, duty-class days, completion) and sends the one-paragraph report.
