## 1. Purpose, category, and the operating law

Continuum is the system of continuity for workplace recovery: the Continuous Recovery Intelligence layer that owns the space between events, where recovery actually happens. The Continuum Effect is adopted as product law: connection reduces preventable uncertainty, reduced uncertainty informs decisions, and informed decisions can contribute to better outcomes. Two corollaries bind everything below. First, the platform informs and people decide: the treating healthcare professional holds diagnosis, treatment, restrictions, fitness and return decisions; the employer holds workplace decisions; Continuum holds continuity. Second, the objective is informed confidence, never promised outcomes: no screen, contract, or marketing sentence guarantees recovery speed, claim reduction, or return dates, and wherever uncertainty exists the platform says so. Prompt 28's Platform Master PRD gives this doctrine its numbered form: the eight platform walls W1 through W8 are promoted into the canon as the working vocabulary of the operating law, numbered, referenced throughout, and inherited by every feature, so a finding can cite a wall the way a suite cites an assertion. Two Master PRD rules enter the canon with them: tiers gate capability, never safety (a subscription tier changes what is available, never what is safe); and the shadow-column rule, that every predictive column exists now and is computed dark, so enabling it is a configuration flag flip rather than a release.

## 2. Architecture

Seven components, one journey. The Recovery Journey Record is the chronological spine every other component reads and writes. Around it: the Worker Application, the Employer and Supervisor Dashboard, the Healthcare Professional Dashboard, the RTW and Occupational Health Dashboard, the Recovery Intelligence Layer, and the Enterprise Integration and Governance Layer. The built demo system already expresses six of these as working surfaces (worker, employer, board, HSE, clinical, platform admin), and the blueprint treats those artifacts as the reference implementation of the screen map, with the board portal standing inside the integration layer as the claims-facing view. Prompt 28's Platform Master PRD composes three net-new AI systems above this architecture without overriding it: the Executive AI Advisor (a grounded Executive Briefing whose every sentence resolves to a fact row or insight or does not ship), the Workforce Digital Twin (a deterministic Tier 0 simulation engine with transparent, graded, challengeable elasticities and the learned twin dark), and the Occupational Intelligence Graph (which separates observed-correlation edges from validated-relationship edges, grades discoveries no higher than Inferred until the closed loop confirms them, and logs the wall filter's cuts). Each stages dark behind its flag per the shadow-column and tier discipline.

Deployment posture: multi-tenant with row-level isolation as the floor and cross-tenant reach only through explicit access grants; all storage and processing in Canadian regions; no processing of identifiable worker data on US infrastructure; staging carries a synthetic tenant so demonstrations never touch real workers. Continuum sits beside authoritative systems, never over them: the EMR remains authoritative for clinical records, the claims system for claims, the safety system for incidents, HR for employment records. Continuum does not duplicate their records; it references them with provenance and holds only what continuity requires. The application stack itself is the first entry in the decision register, because the two governing documents assert different answers, and nothing in this blueprint depends on either.

## 3. User and role map

Roles are data, not code. Six login roles: worker, hse (the RTW and occupational health coordinator), employer_admin (carrying the supervisor and leadership views), physician (the treating healthcare professional seat), board_officer (the claims-facing seat), and continuum_admin. Eleven views resolve onto them: the supervisor and leadership are permission-scoped views within the employer role rather than separate logins in MVP, and the claims professional view is the board seat plus, where the tenant's claims system participates, the Recovery Intelligence panel delivered into that system. Prompt 26 adds two integration views: the SIGMA administrator view, which expresses the customer-side integration contract as configuration (which reference fields flow in, which outcome fields write back, and the identity mapping) and is central to enterprise trust; and the occupational health view aligned to the SIGMA-RH Medical Clinics module, which references the medical record without duplicating it. Adding supervisor or leadership as first-class logins later, or these two integration views, is an insert into the role table, not a refactor.

## 4. The recovery workflow, end to end

Injury identified and recorded in the authoritative safety or claims system; medical assessment; restrictions and plan documented, timestamped on receipt; journey created in Continuum; worker invited with informed consent, with the proxy path standing ready for workers who decline the app, because participation is voluntary and the case runs fully without it; daily priorities and prescribed activities delivered; check-ins capture symptoms, function, confidence, fatigue and duty response in under thirty seconds; modified duties flow through the lifecycle: matched, hazard checked, assigned, accepted by the worker and no one else, active, completed, with a morning acknowledgement each working day recording that the worker knew that day's permitted and prohibited duties; signals connect over time; indicators surface to the coordinator and professional per program rules; the professional reviews and alone moves medical status through the legal transition table; the employer adapts duties inside the restriction envelope; the journey proceeds through graduated return, including backward moves, because recovery is not linear and the state machine permits reassessment in both directions; sign off closes the journey; the completed journey feeds organizational learning as aggregate, de-identified material.

## 6. User stories, compressed to the decisive ones

The worker: I see today's duties and confirm I understand them, so I never guess what is safe; I check in honestly in thirty seconds, because only my care team sees it. The supervisor: I know before the shift what work each recovering worker may perform, without seeing anything medical. The professional: I open an appointment already knowing what changed since last time, and only I can change restrictions or status. The coordinator: my queue tells me who needs attention today and why, and the system refuses to let me assign an unhazard-checked duty or force a worker's acceptance. The claims professional: I see what was filed, what is acknowledged, and what deadline approaches. Leadership: I see whether the organization is supporting recovery, in aggregates, never in individuals. The admin: I can pause a tenant's program in one action and answer what is held about any worker.

## 7. Functional and non-functional requirements

Functional, numbered for acceptance: F1 journey record with full chronology and provenance on every entry; F2 consent gating all worker-sourced capture, with separate video consent, withdrawal through any coordinator, and capture stopping immediately; F3 check-in capture of pain, function, fatigue, confidence, duty response, and free text, worker-scoped; F4 the projection law: every role-facing serialization carries only that role's lawful fields, enforced at the API layer and verified by a standing CI test that fails the build if a clinical field appears in an employer payload, and the same law governs the SIGMA-RH write-back adapter identically, because SIGMA-RH is the employer's system read by employer eyes: outcome fields cross that boundary in functional workflow vocabulary only (a case under clinical review per program rules, a duty plan under review), never as escalation, regression, or plateau, and every projected return-to-work window carries its provenance in the field itself as a clinician-reviewed estimate, with the CI test extended to the write-back channel to assert zero clinical fields, zero intelligence vocabulary, and a provenance label on every estimate, and every Tier 1 predictive output reaches a payload only when four independent conditions hold at once (the tenant compliance flag on, the model enabled, regulatory status cleared, and the caller capability present), enforced server-side in the serializer with the fields omitted rather than nulled and tested in CI, per Prompt 27; F5 the duty lifecycle with the hazard gate and worker-owned acceptance, plus the daily acknowledgement event; F6 the restriction-to-duty matcher proposing only library duties consistent with current restrictions, with the human hazard check disposing; F7 indicator rules with routing to coordinator and professional per program configuration; F8 physician-only medical transitions through the legal table, all else rejected server-side; F9 document generation and tracked submission for board packages, with signed URL access and per-view audit; F10 notifications under the content rule: no health information in any SMS or push, quiet hours enforced, STOP honored; F11 the billing meter stamped by program start and end events; F12 audit on every read of sensitive material and every write everywhere. Non-functional: Canadian data residency; availability appropriate to a daily-use clinical-adjacent tool; accessibility to WCAG AA with the worker surface tested for grade 7 reading level and 44 pixel touch targets; check-in completable in under thirty seconds; analytics performance at 500,000-headcount scale, a two-second first paint and a one-second drill, per Prompt 27; encryption in transit and at rest; tested restore before the first live case; horizontal multi-tenancy without data movement between tenants.

## 8. Data model, major entities

Tenant; User (role as data, org, status); Worker (person-level, minimal); Journey (the case: worker, tenant, state, program timestamps); Assessment (professional-entered, restrictions attached, received timestamp); Restriction (structured, effective and review dates); CheckIn (scores, flags, text, worker-scoped); Activity (prescribed item and completion events); Duty (library reference, class as suitable onsite or standard precaution, lifecycle state, hazard check record, acceptance, daily acknowledgements); Signal (typed observation with source); Indicator (pattern instance: rule, contributing signals, period, state); Insight (role-scoped rendering: what changed, contributors, period, uncertainty, authority); Consent (type, granted, withdrawn, actor); AccessGrant (grantee, subject tenant, scope); Document (type, storage reference, submission and acknowledgement state); Message (thread, participants); AuditEvent (actor, action, object, timestamp); BillingEvent (journey, type, stamp). The Prompt 26 occupational database enters as first-class entities the deterministic match engine queries: Position; Task (with its demand factors); DemandFactor; RestrictionCode (the twenty-two-code taxonomy); DutyCompatibility (a position or task against a restriction code, resolving to blocked, conditional with a named modification, or clear); and RecoveryWindowConfiguration (per injury). Every substantive record carries provenance from the brief's enumerated sources, worker reported through generated by the intelligence layer, and provenance renders in every UI that shows the fact.

## 9. The Recovery Intelligence model

Signals are single observations and are never conclusions; the built check-in, duty feedback and completion events are already signals. Indicators are patterns over signals: the built regression rules (sustained high pain across consecutive check-ins, declining function across days, red-flag language, and now declining confidence paired with declining participation, and repeated discomfort after a specific duty) become the first indicator library, each one a named, versioned, explainable rule. Indicators bring matters to a person; they never diagnose, prescribe, determine fitness, or change restrictions. Insights are the role-appropriate explanations: each insight card shows what changed, which signals contributed, over what period, why it may matter, what remains uncertain, and who holds authority to act, and the platform never shows an unexplained color or score where a decision could follow it. The employer surface receives only functional insights in functional vocabulary; the intelligence vocabulary lives with the coordinator and the professional. Routing language everywhere is per program rules, preserving the boundary that keeps the intelligence layer outside device regulation: the independent professional review is what changes treatment. Prompt 27's CRTW Intelligence PRD formalizes this layer as a thirteen-detector registry (the twelve named detectors plus Rehabilitation Benefit), each tagged Tier 0 or Tier 1 with every Tier 1 element naming its Tier 0 fallback and the existing regression rules serving as that fallback, with a k-anonymity guard at k_min default 5 applied at the point of detection so a suppressed cohort never even generates an insight, and its Recommendation Card is field for field the six-part explainable insight anatomy Prompt 22 filed as remediation R1, so the R1 build implements Craig's object rather than a parallel one. Prompt 28 extends the explainability law upward: the Organizational Health Index that headlines the executive surface is a composite score sitting where decisions follow, so its formula, components, and weights display wherever the index does or it does not ship, because an unexplained organizational score is the same sin as an unexplained worker score one level up. Prompt 28 also assigns the specification gap: of the seventeen named intelligence surfaces the four beyond the CRTW PRD's thirteen (Supervisor Intelligence, Job Task Intelligence expanded, Organizational Health, and the Benchmarking, Heat Map and Predictive layers as first-class surfaces) are each written to the fifteen-part template at CRTW-PRD depth before their phase begins, since a catalogue entry is not buildable.

## 10. Permissions, consent, and privacy

The permission matrix from the built system is adopted as the blueprint matrix: worker sees self entirely; professional sees full clinical for granted cases; coordinator sees full clinical plus functional plus board for tenant cases; employer sees functional only, with diagnosis, symptoms, notes, scores, video and consent state structurally absent; claims sees submitted material; leadership sees aggregates; admin sees operational metadata and never case content. Consent is informed, layered (check-ins; video separately, off by default), withdrawable through a human, and its absence is architectural: the proxy path keeps the journey whole. Data minimization is a review gate: any proposed field must name the decision it improves. Access logging, change history, document history, and source attribution are table stakes from section 8. Retention is configurable per record class, shortest for video, longest for audit, with statutory minimums confirmed by counsel before first live data. Two Prompt 27 repairs bind here. Supervisor-cohort operational analytics are named in the tenant's electronic monitoring policy, stating the coaching-not-discipline purpose and the appealability the detector already provides, because analytics computed over a supervisor's span is workplace monitoring of the supervisor as an employee under Ontario's electronic monitoring obligation. And check-in completion renders only at site and tenant aggregate for employer-side roles, remaining at span grain for the coordinator and admin who already hold individual case access, with the standing sentence riding along wherever any employer-side surface shows it: participation is voluntary and non-participation is never a performance matter.

## 11. Notifications and escalation

Notification content law: an SMS or push may say only that something awaits, never what. Quiet hours 8am to 9pm worker-local, STOP honored, channel preferences per user. Escalation logic: indicator fires, coordinator queue and professional are notified per program rules, employer receives the functional consequence only (duty plan under review), resolution is recorded with the reviewer, and one signal produces one escalation, with re-raise only on genuinely new signal, the semantics already proven in the built system.

## 12. Documents and integration architecture

Documents are generated from the journey record (assessment summaries, restriction notices, board packages, clearance letters), stored once, served by signed URL, and audited per view. Integrations, MVP: intake from the tenant's incident or claims system at the level that system supports, file-based where necessary, with tracked outbound board submissions through per-board adapters and manual filing as the honest fallback. Phase two: the claims-system Recovery Intelligence panel as a delivered integration, safety and HR directory sync, and FHIR. The FHIR and SMART on FHIR strategy, which the brief adds to the program: adopt FHIR resource mappings for the clinical seam (DocumentReference for assessments and letters, Condition and CarePlan for the clinical context Continuum references rather than owns, Task and ServiceRequest for restriction-driven workflow) and SMART on FHIR launch so the professional dashboard can open inside an EMR session in phase two or three; nothing in MVP depends on FHIR, and the pilot's clinical partner workflow runs on Continuum's own professional seat, which keeps the pilot honest about what is integrated today.

SIGMA-RH enterprise integration, the SIGMA chapter from Prompt 26: the anchor facts are GardaWorld's confirmed system of record across more than 35,000 Canadian employees, a five-module OHS suite, Azure with per-customer isolation and Canadian residency, and a connector-first reality rather than a documented public API. The integration is an asymmetric data contract: Continuum consumes a shallow reference set (worker, employer, claim, and position identifiers) and returns intelligence the claim record cannot compute (functional recovery milestones, modified-duty participation, projected return-to-work windows carrying their provenance, and functionalized workflow states), so the system of record wants the connection because it makes its own data more valuable. The contract degrades across three honest states: ideal is a bidirectional REST integration with SSO federation; pragmatic is a Power Automate connector plus scheduled file exchange; pilot keeps the manual export already in force, so no promise here depends on an unconfirmed vendor capability. The occupational database is stated with canon numbers so every surface that cites them agrees: 45 positions, 50 tasks across 27 demand factors, 14 injuries with recovery-window configuration, 209 pre-matched duties across 26 categories, and the 22-code restriction taxonomy. The match engine is deterministic and buildable as specified: incompatible codes block outright, occasional-demand conflicts mark a duty conditional with the modification surfaced, and candidates rank by essential-demand coverage weighted by duty class, which upgrades the demo's Scene 8 vignette from illustration to a working engine. Two vendor dependencies (the REST or SOAP API beyond the connector, and SAML or Entra federation) and the diligence artifacts (the SOC 2 report and the ISO 27001 certificate) are carried in the decision register as D9, D10, and D11; none blocks the pilot.

Board submission channels, the Prompt 29 matrix: all twelve Canadian compensation boards were checked, and three expose machine submission channels (WSIB Ontario B2B XML with a returned confirmation number, WorkSafeBC ISSv3, and WCB Alberta C040 file import) while the other nine are portal-assist only, Yukon on paper; no Canadian board publishes a claim-data retrieval API. The Ontario pilot board is therefore specified as a true machine-to-machine B2B XML integration rather than an assemble-a-draft hand-off, with the human-authorizes-the-send gate unchanged: the human clicks file and the adapter transmits. Because no board offers retrieval, rebate actuals enter through the system of record (the better source under the data-ownership contract) or a customer-supplied export, and every rebate figure stays a labeled estimate until such an input arrives, which is W8 applied to money that has not landed. Engineering goes only where a channel exists (certify three, portal-assist nine), and the matrix carries a quarterly re-verification cadence because board digital services change.

Compliance and Rebate Intelligence, Addendum A adopted by Prompt 30: a Tier 0 capability that feeds the record and never becomes it, post-pilot except for its schema-first elements. It prepares and hands off where SIGMA already files (Quebec, Alberta, BC) and offers assisted preparation only where the confirmed gap exists (the Ontario Form 7), because two filers is a compliance hazard, and it never auto-submits, the human files always. The Form 7 tension between a legally required injury description and wall W1 is handled by field routing (clinician-authored clinical fields flow to the board on the document and never into employer analytics) and is marked open for counsel and privacy review under the closing rule that if a field cannot be assembled lawfully, the wall wins. A deadline clock with version-stamped jurisdiction rules, a rebate engine held to a no-fabricated-evidence acceptance criterion, and a reconciliation that keeps every figure a labeled estimate until an actual lands complete the capability, which turns the ROI story from money we estimate we saved into the fine you did not pay and the rebate you earned.

## 13. AI boundaries

The brief's two lists are adopted verbatim as policy. Permitted: summarization, pattern recognition, organization, trend identification, communication support, role-specific explanation, attention prioritization, and flagging material for professional review. Prohibited independently: diagnosis, treatment, restriction changes, fitness determinations, return approvals, benefit denials, employment decisions, legal determinations, and certainty theater. Operationally: generated drafts are coordinator-facing and human-approved before any worker sees derived content; no identifiable data leaves Canadian processing for any model call; every generated artifact is provenance-tagged as generated by the intelligence layer; and uncertainty is stated in the artifact itself. Prompt 28 makes one boundary explicit against the Master PRD's check-in avatar: nothing generative speaks to a worker. The shipping check-in avatar is a scripted, deterministic conversational rendering of the check-in schema, a guided flow whose every prompt and branch is authored, reviewed copy at grade 7 under the vocabulary laws, delivering conversational warmth and the thirty-second capture with no generative model in the loop; it collects and routes per program rules and never advises, reassures diagnostically, or improvises. A genuinely generative conversational layer, if ever wanted, stages like every other regulated capability: built dark behind its own flag and enabled only through governance.

## 14. Audit and explainability

Every write everywhere, every read of clinical material, every video view, every permission change, every flag flip, every transition, every submission: actor, action, object, time, and where relevant the before and after. Explainability requirement: any surfaced indicator or insight must be traceable on screen to its contributing signals and rule version, and any AI-generated text must be identifiable as such. The audit trail is a first-class product surface for the admin and a queryable record for access requests and disputes.

## 15. Phasing

MVP is the pilot spine: journey record, worker application with consent and check-in v2 (fatigue, confidence, daily acknowledgement), duty lifecycle with matching and the hazard gate, coordinator dashboard with the attention queue and instrumentation, professional dashboard with transitions and video review, employer functional dashboard, board submission tracking, notifications under the content rule, admin with pause, grants and the billing meter, audit throughout, and the CI projection test. Phase two: the delivered claims-panel integration, FHIR read paths and SMART launch, leadership analytics maturation, supervisor login, first-aid and near-miss capture where the tenant wants it, expanded indicator library. Future, each behind a flag and a governance gate: pose analysis, predictive duration and risk models, benchmarking network effects across tenants on de-identified aggregates. The strategic discipline gate from the brief, ten questions ending in MVP or later or outside, is adopted as the standing feature-review checklist, and the built demo already models the discipline: prediction appears only as labeled roadmap material. The July 23 pilot minimum ruled by Gary (Prompt 31) runs entirely on this demo family with the coordinator as the proxy hub: enrollment and consent on paper, coordinator-entered proxy check-ins with the escalation rule, clinician restrictions keyed as received, duties matched from the employer list and hazard checked on site, and the Ontario Form 7 filed on the human path through WSIB eServices, with zero backend dependency, so Phase 1 carries no D1 blocker and the B2B adapter and the Part II backend are Phase 2.

## 16. Risks and dependencies

Principal risks: regulatory drift if intelligence language slides from routing to recommending, mitigated by the phrasing law and marketing review; trust failure if any employer leak ever occurs, mitigated by the structural projection and the CI test, which is the single most important test in the codebase; pilot friction if the check-in exceeds thirty seconds or duties arrive without acknowledgement clarity; integration overreach if the SIGMA panel is promised as live before intake maturity; and counsel dependencies on retention, video access defaults, and Ontario instrument mapping. Dependencies: clinical partner availability for the professional seat, the tenant's duty library quality for matching, and Twilio or equivalent under the content rule.

## 17. Assumption register

Assumed, and flagged as assumptions per the brief: the treating healthcare professional in the pilot is a physician and the transition table is physician-only, per the v2 ruling, with physiotherapist and nurse practitioner authority a decision, not an assumption; the supervisor and leadership operate as views within the employer login for MVP; the pilot claims-professional experience is submission tracking, not the embedded panel; consent text and retention schedules await counsel; the Ontario pilot governs first-launch statutory mapping; and the demonstration cast and numbers, while canon-locked, are synthetic.

## 18. Acceptance criteria for MVP capabilities

Each MVP capability ships only when: consent gate provably blocks capture when absent and withdrawal stops it immediately; a check-in completes in under thirty seconds on a mid-range phone and lands with correct scoping; the CI projection test passes and a deliberately injected clinical field fails the build; a duty cannot reach assigned without a hazard check record, cannot be accepted by anyone but its worker, and records daily acknowledgements; an indicator fires from its rule inputs, routes per configuration, resolves once, and re-raises only on new signal; an illegal medical transition is rejected server-side and a legal one audits with actor and states; a board package generates, files, and tracks acknowledgement with deadlines computed per board; a tenant pause stops capture tenant-wide within one minute; an access request produces the complete plain-language export; and every criterion above leaves its trace in the audit log.

## 19. Development sequence

Foundations first: tenancy, identity, roles, audit, consent. Then the journey record and the professional seat, because restrictions are the ignition key. Then the worker application and check-in, the coordinator dashboard and duty lifecycle, the employer projection with its CI test, notifications, board submission tracking, the indicator rules, the admin surface with the meter, and hardening: restore tests, load, accessibility, and the pilot instrumentation readout. The built portal family remains the design reference at every step, and each backend milestone should be demonstrable by pointing the corresponding portal at it.

## 20. The decision register, and the first question

Open decisions, to be resolved one at a time per the brief: D1, the application stack, where the v2 specification assumes NestJS with PostgreSQL and React Native while the ratified series baseline is Next.js with Supabase and Capacitor, and every week of backend work multiplies the cost of deciding late; D2, whether physiotherapists or nurse practitioners ever hold transition authority, and for which transitions; D3, FHIR's phase placement against pilot timeline; D4, the coordinator's default access to consented video, which counsel flagged; D5, retention schedules per record class; D6, the Ontario privacy instrument mapping for the pilot consent text; D7, the scope promised to GardaWorld for the claims panel in pilot materials; D8, the marketing boundary for the word intelligence against the device posture.

Per the brief's instruction to ask unresolved questions one at a time, here is the first, and only the first:

**Decision 1. Which application stack governs the mainline build: the NestJS, PostgreSQL and React Native stack asserted in the v2 wiring specification, or the Next.js, Supabase and Capacitor stack ratified earlier in the series?** Everything else in this blueprint is stack-agnostic and stands either way; the answer only determines who types what, starting when.
